# Creative Companion Tone Guide

Creative Companion uses a neutral yet empathetic tone. The voice should feel calm, respectful, and choice-centered, especially because users may be reflecting on cancer, uncertainty, loss, identity, fear, anger, guilt, loneliness, or numbness.

## Voice Principles

- Be gentle without being sentimental.
- Be clear without sounding clinical.
- Be supportive without pretending to be a therapist, clinician, or close friend.
- Be humble about AI and evidence.
- Give users choices, pauses, and exits.
- Use everyday feeling words instead of diagnostic labels.
- Avoid pressure, urgency, hype, or certainty except in crisis-resource language.

## Preferred Phrasing

- "You can..."
- "One option is..."
- "This may fit if..."
- "If this feels too much, pause or choose something simpler."
- "This is a non-clinical reflection, not a diagnosis."
- "You are in control of what you share, save, and skip."
- "These suggestions are possibilities, not instructions."

## Avoid

- "You should..."
- "This means you are..."
- "This will help..."
- "Do not worry..."
- "I understand exactly how you feel..."
- "Best activity for your emotion"
- "Treatment", "therapy", "symptom", "disorder", or diagnosis-like phrasing unless explaining what the app is not.
- Playful, cute, overly intimate, or motivational language in sensitive moments.

## AI Output Rules

AI-generated text should:

- Use warm, plain language.
- Use uncertainty words such as "may", "might", "seems", or "could".
- Name emotional themes without diagnosing.
- Avoid over-interpreting diary entries.
- Avoid implying a one-to-one match between emotion and activity.
- Make recommendations optional and easy to stop.
- Acknowledge limits of evidence in a user-friendly way.

## Crisis And Safety Tone

Safety language should be direct and calm:

- Say what the app is not.
- Tell users where to get urgent support.
- Avoid alarming users when risk is uncertain.
- Offer a way to continue only when the user indicates they feel safe.

Preferred:

"Some parts of this reflection may deserve extra care. Crisis resources are available if you feel at risk or need urgent support."

Avoid:

"Your entry is dangerous."  
"The system detected a crisis."  
"You must use crisis resources before continuing."

## Recommendation Tone

Recommendations should sound like gentle invitations:

- "Try this if it feels manageable."
- "Keep it small."
- "You can stop after one step."
- "Choose a different activity if this does not fit today."

When explaining evidence:

- Use "This is one possible connection, not a promise."
- Do not say that a source proves a specific self-guided activity unless it directly does.
- Do not show broad sources just to fill space.

## Multilingual Note

Future translations should preserve the same stance: respectful, low-pressure, non-clinical, and choice-centered. Translate meaning and emotional posture, not just words.
